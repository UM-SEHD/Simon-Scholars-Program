Program Vision

Cultivating the Next Generation
of Sport & Business Leaders

The Simon Scholars Program is an elite co-curricular experiential learning initiative designed to cultivate innovative, solution-driven thinkers prepared to excel in sport, business, and entrepreneurial environments.

Through immersive, collaborative experiences that integrate design thinking, professional mentorship, and real-world problem-solving, students develop the mindset, confidence, and strategic capacity needed to contribute meaningfully to future organizations and industries.

Grounded in reciprocal mentoring and cross-generational collaboration, the program positions students to thrive in dynamic professional settings and prepares them to navigate complex challenges with creativity, rigor, and purpose.

Program Participants

Scholars & Fellows

The program distinguishes between two tiers of participants — each with a distinct role in the journey from selection to professional immersion.

Foundation Tier

Simon Scholars

Simon Scholars are all students selected to participate in the program. Scholars engage in the core developmental components, building foundational skills in problem-solving, innovation, communication, and strategic thinking.

  • Required participation in all Innovation Labs
  • Engagement in the Simon Development Series
  • Access to professional mentorship and peer collaboration
  • Invited to observe and learn at The Simon Summit
S
Elite Advancement

Simon Fellows

Simon Fellows are the highest-performing participants, chosen from the Scholars cohort based on performance, engagement, and demonstrated growth during the Innovation Labs. They advance to the program's culminating experiences.

  • Selected to compete at The Simon Summit
  • Access to the Simon Externship Experience
  • Real-world professional immersion opportunities
  • Priority consideration for career placements
F

Program Structure

Five Phases of a
Comprehensive Journey

The program is structured across five key phases that create a complete developmental arc — from competitive selection through real-world professional immersion.

01

Phase One

Selection Process

Students are selected as Simon Scholars through a competitive, multi-step application process assessing motivation, readiness, creativity, and alignment with program expectations. 12 Scholars are chosen from a competitive applicant pool.

Deadline: Jan 5, 2026 · Notifications: Jan 14, 2026
02

Phase Two

Innovation Labs

All Simon Scholars participate in required, seminar-style Innovation Labs focused on design thinking, strategic problem-solving, and collaboration with industry professionals. Performance in Labs determines advancement to Simon Fellow status.

Jan 20 – Mar 17, 2026 · Every other Monday, 6:30–9:30 PM
03

Phase Three

The Simon Summit

Simon Fellows compete in The Simon Summit, delivering 10–12-minute strategic solutions to a real-world sport or business challenge before a panel of respected industry leaders. Scholars observe and continue learning from peer presentations.

March 26, 2026
04

Phase Four

Simon Development Series

Both Scholars and Fellows participate in professional development sessions led by sport and business leaders. Workshops focus on leadership presence, personal branding, networking, career strategy, and ecosystem building.

Post-Summit · Spring 2026
05

Phase Five · Fellows Only

Simon Externship Experience

Reserved for Simon Fellows. This immersive professional opportunity bridges the gap between classroom learning and real-world practice — placing Fellows inside partner organizations to apply their skills in live industry environments.

Curated by the Simons & Partner Organizations

Expectations

Full Commitment.
Full Reward.

Students selected for the Simon Scholars Program are expected to commit fully. This commitment is what separates the experience from a standard class — and what makes the recognition meaningful.

Scholars who successfully complete all phases receive formal recognition, résumé-building experiences, and priority consideration for Simon Fellow Externship placements and professional opportunities.

Upon Completion

Formal recognition, résumé-building credentials, and priority consideration for Externship placements and related professional opportunities within the Simon network.

  • Attend all required Innovation Labs and program events
  • Demonstrate professionalism in all interactions with faculty, peers, and industry professionals
  • Wear business attire for designated sessions and The Simon Summit
  • Engage actively in collaborative learning, group work, and reciprocal mentoring
  • Complete all required deliverables, including reflections, presentations, and externship materials
  • Maintain strong communication and follow-through throughout the program
